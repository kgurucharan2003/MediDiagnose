How to Obtain Your Health Data

Age

Enter your age in years. This should be your current age as of today.

Sex

Enter 1 if you are male and 0 if you are female. This is important because heart disease risk can vary between genders.

Chest Pain Type (cp)

Chest pain type ranges from 0 to 3, where:
  • 0: Typical angina
  • 1: Atypical angina
  • 2: Non-anginal pain
  • 3: Asymptomatic
Consult your doctor to determine which type applies to you.

Resting Blood Pressure (trestbps)

Measure your resting blood pressure with a home monitor or at a clinic. This value should be in millimeters of mercury (mm Hg).

Cholesterol Level (chol)

This value is obtained from a blood test and should be in milligrams per deciliter (mg/dL). You can get it tested at a clinic or use a home test kit.

Fasting Blood Sugar (fbs)

If your fasting blood sugar is greater than 120 mg/dL, enter 1; otherwise, enter 0. This value can be obtained from a blood test after fasting for 8-12 hours.

Resting ECG (restecg)

An electrocardiogram (ECG) test records the electrical activity of your heart. This test is typically done at a doctor's office or hospital.

Maximum Heart Rate Achieved (thalach)

This is the highest heart rate you achieve during exercise. It can be measured during a stress test or by using a heart rate monitor while exercising.

Exercise Induced Angina (exang)

Enter 1 if you experience angina (chest pain) during exercise, and 0 if you do not. This is usually determined during a stress test.

ST Depression Induced by Exercise (oldpeak)

This value represents the difference in the ST segment of your ECG during exercise compared to rest. It's measured during a stress test.

Slope of the Peak Exercise ST Segment (slope)

This value indicates the slope of the ST segment during peak exercise in an ECG. It ranges from 0 to 2:
  • 0: Upsloping
  • 1: Flat
  • 2: Downsloping
This is usually determined during an exercise stress test. Consult your healthcare provider for accurate measurement.

Number of Major Vessels (ca)

This value represents the number of major blood vessels (0-3) that are colored by fluoroscopy (a type of medical imaging). This test is often conducted in a hospital setting during a cardiac evaluation.

Thalassemia (thal)

Thalassemia refers to a blood disorder involving less than normal oxygen-carrying protein (hemoglobin) in red blood cells. The values typically range from:
  • 1: Normal
  • 2: Fixed defect
  • 3: Reversible defect
This is typically diagnosed through a blood test. Consult your doctor for this value if applicable.
